## 📖 About this repository

This repository brings together the exercises, rewritten examples, and small projects I'm building while studying (or re-studying) programming logic and algorithms using JavaScript as the teaching language.

I've been passionate about technology since I was very young — around 10 years old I was already curious about how things worked under the hood, and that curiosity never really left. Over time, I drifted a bit from the deeper theoretical foundations of programming to focus on other areas, and today I feel a bit "rusty" on logic. This repository is my way of polishing that foundation again, going through the fundamentals carefully, exercise by exercise.

The goal here isn't just to practice code — it's to document the process. Every chapter, exercise, or small project from the book that I solve gets recorded here, serving both as a progress log and as part of my presentation as a developer on GitHub.

## 💡 Motivation

- Reconnect with programming logic and algorithms after some time away from pure theory.
- Consolidate fundamentals that support any language or stack, not just JavaScript.
- Create a public, honest record of my progress — including mistakes, attempts, and refactors.
- Share my journey with anyone else who's starting out or reviewing their own studies.

**Lógica de Programação e Algoritmos com JavaScript**
Author: Edécio Fernando Iepsen
Publisher: Novatec
Edition: 2nd revised and expanded edition

The book is an introduction to computer programming, with examples and exercises designed for beginners, using JavaScript as the teaching language.

## 🗂 Repository structure

> Suggested structure — may evolve as the study progresses.

```
.
├── chapters/
│   ├── 01-introduction/
│   ├── 02-variables-and-types/
│   ├── 03-conditional-structures/
│   ├── 04-loops/
│   ├── 05-arrays-and-matrices/
│   ├── 06-functions/
│   └── ...
├── exercises/
│   └── (exercises proposed by the book, solved)
├── projects/
│   └── (final projects and bigger challenges)
├── README.md
├── README.pt-BR.md
└── README.en.md
```

Each chapter folder contains commented examples and solutions to the proposed exercises, following the order of the book.

## 🧭 How to navigate

1. Go to the `chapters/` folder to follow the study in the order presented by the book.
2. Each solved exercise has a comment at the top of the file explaining the proposed problem.
3. Larger projects (end of sections or of the book) live under `projects/`.
